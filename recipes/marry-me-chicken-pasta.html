Marry Me Chicken Pasta

← Home

Description

Marry Me Chicken Pasta is a creamy and flavorful pasta dish made with tender chicken in a rich, savory sauce. The dish features seared chicken breasts simmered in a luscious sauce of sun-dried tomatoes, garlic, heavy cream, and Parmesan cheese. The combination of these ingredients creates a velvety texture with a perfect balance of tangy, cheesy, and garlicky flavors. Served over al dente pasta, it is a comforting and indulgent meal.

The name "Marry Me Chicken" comes from the idea that its irresistible taste could inspire a marriage proposal. While the exact origins are unclear, the dish has gained popularity as a romantic and restaurant-quality meal that is surprisingly easy to prepare at home. Whether served for a special occasion or a simple weeknight dinner, this pasta dish is sure to impress with its rich, creamy sauce and bold flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (sliced or pounded thin)
  • 200g pasta (fettuccine, penne, or spaghetti)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • ½ cup sun-dried tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup chicken broth
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh basil or parsley (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Season chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat and sear chicken until golden brown on both sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, melt butter and sauté garlic and red pepper flakes until fragrant.
  4. Add sun-dried tomatoes, heavy cream, chicken broth, and Parmesan cheese. Stir until smooth.
  5. Return the chicken to the pan, simmer in the sauce for 5–7 minutes, and let it thicken.
  6. Boil salted water and cook pasta until al dente, then drain and reserve ½ cup of pasta water.
  7. Toss the pasta in the sauce, adding reserved pasta water if needed for consistency.
  8. Slice the chicken and serve over pasta, garnishing with fresh basil or parsley.
